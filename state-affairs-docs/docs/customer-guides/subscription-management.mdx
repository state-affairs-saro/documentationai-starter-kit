# Subscription Management

# Understanding Your State Affairs Subscriptions

## Do You Have Multiple Subscriptions?

One of the most common sources of confusion is when customers unknowingly have multiple active subscriptions. This guide helps you understand what subscriptions you have and how to manage them.

---

## Types of State Affairs Subscriptions

### 1. Print + Digital Subscription
**What's Included:**
- Physical newspaper delivered to your mailing address
- Full digital access to online platform
- Mobile app access
- Email alerts and notifications
- Search and archive access

**Best For:** Organizations that want both physical copies for offices and digital access for remote teams

**Typical Cost:** $28-30/month per subscription

---

### 2. Digital-Only Subscription
**What's Included:**
- Full digital access to online platform
- Mobile app access
- Email alerts and notifications
- Search and archive access
- NO physical newspaper delivery

**Best For:** Individuals or organizations that prefer all-digital access

**Typical Cost:** $20-25/month

---

### 3. Print-Only Subscription
**What's Included:**
- Physical newspaper delivered to your mailing address
- NO digital platform access

**Best For:** Organizations that only need physical copies

**Less Common:** Most customers choose Print+Digital for full access

---

## How to Check Which Subscriptions You Have

### Red Flags You Might Have Multiple Subscriptions:

1. **You're charged more than once per month**
   - Check your credit card or bank statements
   - Look for multiple charges from State Affairs

2. **You receive more newspapers than you expect**
   - Are you getting 2 or more copies delivered?
   - This often indicates multiple print subscriptions

3. **Your charges don't match what you remember signing up for**
   - Expected $28/month but being charged $56?
   - That's likely two subscriptions

4. **You have subscriptions under different email addresses**
   - Check if you signed up with work email AND personal email
   - Each creates a separate subscription

### To Verify Your Subscriptions:

**Option 1: Check Your Email**
- Search your inbox for "State Affairs" + "invoice"
- Count how many DIFFERENT subscription invoices you receive each month
- Look at the invoice details - each will show subscription type

**Option 2: Contact Support**
Email: billing@stateaffairs.com

Include in your email:
- Your organization name
- All email addresses that might be associated with accounts
- Your question: "Can you tell me how many active subscriptions I have?"

**We'll reply within 24 hours with:**
- Complete list of your active subscriptions
- What each subscription includes
- Monthly cost of each
- Suggestion on which to keep

## Common Scenarios & Solutions

### Scenario 1: "I thought I only had one subscription, but I'm charged twice"

**What Probably Happened:**
- You may have signed up for Print+Digital AND Digital-Only
- Or someone else at your organization created a second subscription
- Or you have one subscription for each of multiple publications

**Solution:**
1. Contact billing@stateaffairs.com
2. Request a complete subscription list
3. Decide which subscription(s) to keep
4. We'll cancel the others and prevent future billing

**Important:** We'll write off any outstanding balance for cancelled subscriptions. Previously collected payments are non-refundable, but you won't be charged going forward.

---

### Scenario 2: "I'm receiving two newspapers but only want one"

**What Probably Happened:**
- You have two Print+Digital subscriptions
- OR you have a Print-Only AND a Print+Digital subscription
- Each one sends you a physical copy

**Solution:**
1. Email support@stateaffairs.com with:
   - Your mailing address
   - How many copies you're receiving
   - How many you actually need
2. We'll identify the duplicate subscriptions
3. We'll cancel the extras
4. We'll contact the printer to stop duplicate deliveries

**Timeline:** It may take 1-2 billing cycles for delivery to fully stop due to print schedules.

---

### Scenario 3: "I have subscriptions for multiple people at my organization"

**This is Normal!** Many organizations have subscriptions for:
- Different team members
- Different departments
- Different office locations

**What You Need to Know:**
- Each subscription is independent
- Each has its own login credentials
- Each is billed separately (unless you have a group plan)

**To Manage Multiple Users:**
- Consider a group/enterprise plan for better pricing
- Contact your account representative
- We can consolidate billing while keeping separate access

---

### Scenario 4: "I cancelled my subscription but was still charged"

**What Probably Happened:**
- The cancellation wasn't processed before the next billing cycle
- You cancelled ONE subscription but have another active
- Your cancellation email wasn't received

**Solution:**
1. Forward your cancellation confirmation (if you have one) to billing@stateaffairs.com
2. Include details about the charge
3. We'll:
   - Verify the cancellation status
   - Process immediate cancellation if needed
   - Refund or credit the charge if appropriate

## How to Cancel a Subscription

### Step 1: Identify Which Subscription to Cancel
- Make sure you know WHICH subscription you want to cancel
- If you have multiple, specify which one(s) to keep

### Step 2: Email Your Cancellation Request

**Send to:** billing@stateaffairs.com

**Include:**
- Your name
- Organization name  
- Email address associated with the subscription
- Which subscription you want to cancel
  - If you have multiple, specify: "Cancel my Digital-Only subscription but keep Print+Digital"

### Step 3: Confirmation

You'll receive:
- Confirmation email within 24-48 hours
- Details on when cancellation takes effect
- Information about any final charges
- When your access will end

### Important Cancellation Details:

**Billing:**
- Cancellations are processed at the end of your current billing period
- You'll have access until the period you've paid for ends
- No mid-period refunds (you keep access for the full month you paid for)

**Digital Access:**
- Remains active until the end of your paid period
- Login will be deactivated on the cancellation effective date

**Print Delivery:**
- May take 1-2 billing cycles to fully stop
- Due to printer schedules and mailing timelines
- Contact us if you're still receiving papers after 4 weeks

## Understanding Your Invoice

### What to Look For:

**1. Invoice Number**
- Unique identifier for each bill
- Format: Year + Number (e.g., 2025067432)

**2. Service Period**
- Shows exactly what dates you're being charged for
- Example: "Jun. 19, 2025 - Jul. 18, 2025"

**3. Subscription Type**
- States whether it's Print, Digital, or Print+Digital
- If not clearly stated, contact billing for clarification

**4. Amount**
- Should match your subscription tier
- Changes may occur due to annual price increases or subscription changes

## Autopay & Subscription Management

### How Autopay Works:

1. You provide payment information
2. Your card is charged automatically each billing period
3. You receive an invoice email for your records
4. Repeat monthly

### Benefits of Autopay:
- Never miss a payment
- Uninterrupted service
- Convenience

### Potential Issues:
- If you have multiple subscriptions, ALL charge automatically
- System migrations can cause catch-up charges for missed payments
- Card expirations cause failed payments

### Managing Autopay:

**To Update Payment Method:**
- Email billing@stateaffairs.com
- We'll guide you through secure payment update process
- Never email credit card details

**To Disable Autopay:**
- Email billing@stateaffairs.com with your request
- You'll receive manual invoices to pay each month
- Risk of service interruption if invoices aren't paid promptly

**Caught Up by Surprise Charges?**
- This sometimes happens after system migrations
- Contact billing@stateaffairs.com immediately
- We can review charges and set up payment plans if needed

## Public Notice Billing vs. Subscription Billing

### What are Public Notices?

Public notices are legal advertisements that organizations must publish. These are separate from subscriptions.

**Examples:**
- Legal proceedings
- Government announcements
- Public meeting notices
- Bidding opportunities

### Why You Received a Public Notice Invoice:

If you requested publication of a public notice, you'll receive a separate invoice for that service. This is:
- **One-time billing** (not recurring)
- **Different from your subscription fee**
- **Sent to the organization requesting publication**

### How to Tell the Difference:

**Subscription Invoice:**
- Monthly recurring charge
- Shows service period (e.g., Jan 1-31)
- Lists subscription type
- Same amount each month (usually)

**Public Notice Invoice:**
- One-time charge
- References specific publication date
- Includes notice details
- Variable amount based on size/placement

## Proactive Subscription Management Tips

### 1. Annual Review
- Once a year, review ALL State Affairs charges on your accounts
- Verify you still need each subscription
- Cancel any that are no longer needed

### 2. Centralize Billing
- Have one person manage all subscriptions for your organization
- Keep a master list of who has access
- Track billing email addresses

### 3. Update When Employees Leave
- Cancel subscriptions for departed employees
- Don't let unused subscriptions accumulate

### 4. Monitor Your Email
- Watch for subscription confirmation emails
- If you didn't sign up, investigate immediately
- Check for multiple accounts under different email addresses

### 5. Budget for Annual Increases
- Subscription prices may increase annually
- You'll be notified in advance
- Budget accordingly to avoid surprises
