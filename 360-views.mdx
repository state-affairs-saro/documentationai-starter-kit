# 360 Views: AI-Powered Legislative Intelligence

<Badge tier="enterprise">Enterprise Only</Badge>

Generate comprehensive, AI-powered reports that analyze legislation, hearings, and news across all 50 states with natural language prompts.

## What are 360 Views?

360 Views is State Affairs' advanced AI feature that creates comprehensive legislative intelligence reports based on your natural language questions. Instead of manually searching through bills, hearings, and news across multiple states, you can ask questions in plain English and receive a complete analysis with visualizations, bill recommendations, legislator insights, and related news—all in seconds.

**Think of it as:** Your AI research assistant that instantly analyzes legislative activity across all 50 states.

## Key Features

### Natural Language Prompts
Ask questions in plain English:
- "Show me bills related to the regulation and taxation of ready-to-drink alcoholic beverages across different states."
- "Summarize hearings on franchise-fee structures this month."
- "Identify recent legislative proposals addressing AI governance."

### Comprehensive Reports
Each 360 View includes:
- **AI-Generated Summary** - Key themes and trends synthesized by AI
- **Heat Map** - Visual state-by-state activity intensity  
- **Recommended Bills** - Relevant legislation sorted by status (Introduced, Chamber, Passed, Failed)
- **Active Legislators** - Key lawmakers involved, with party affiliation
- **Featured Hearings** - Upcoming committee meetings with visual previews
- **Related News** - Recent articles and developments
- **Schedules** - Upcoming hearing dates organized by state

### Interactive Heat Map
- **Visual intensity:** Darker blue = more activity
- **Filter by:** Bills, Hearings, News (toggle on/off)
- **At-a-glance:** See which states have the most legislative activity

### Bill Status Tracking
Bills organized by status with progress bars:
- **Introduced:** 63 bills
- **Chamber:** 10 bills  
- **Passed:** 26 bills
- **Failed:** 16 bills

### Legislator Intelligence
See which lawmakers are most active on your topic:
- State and party affiliation (D/R badges)
- Number of relevant bills sponsored
- Positions calculated by AI based on bill activity

### Saved Views
- All 360 Views are automatically saved
- Access previous reports from the left sidebar
- Build a knowledge base over time

## How to Create a 360 View

### Step 1: Navigate to 360 Views
1. Click **360 Views** in the top navigation
2. You'll see the 360 Views interface with a prompt input box

### Step 2: Enter Your Prompt
Type your question or research topic in the search box. Be specific about what you want to analyze.

**Examples:**
- "Show me bills related to [topic] across different states"
- "Analyze trends in [policy area] legislation"
- "Summarize hearings on [issue] this month"
- "Identify recent proposals addressing [topic]"

**Pro Tips:**
- Be specific about timeframes (e.g., "this month", "last 90 days")
- Include policy areas (e.g., "taxation", "regulation", "licensing")
- Mention specific aspects (e.g., "labeling requirements", "distribution models")

### Step 3: Click "Generate 360 View"
The AI will analyze:
- Bills across all 50 states
- Recent hearings
- News articles  
- Legislator activity
- Trending topics

**Processing time:** 10-30 seconds depending on complexity

### Step 4: Explore Your Results
Your 360 View will include:

**1. AI Summary (Top Left)**
- State-level analysis
- Regulatory trends
- Key themes

**2. Heat Map (Center)**
- Toggle: Bills, Hearings, News
- Darker = more activity
- Click states for details

**3. Bill Recommendations (Bottom Left)**
- Organized by status
- Click "View All" for complete list
- Progress bars show status breakdown

**4. Active Legislators (Bottom Left)**
- Key lawmakers on this topic
- Party affiliation
- Number of bills

**5. Featured Hearings (Bottom Center)**
- Upcoming committee meetings
- Visual previews with state flags
- Dates and committees

**6. Related News (Bottom Center)**
- Recent articles with images
- Headlines and dates
- Click to read full article

**7. State Details (Right Sidebar)**
- Click any state on the map
- See bills, hearings, news for that state
- Quick state-specific overview

## Best Practices

### Writing Effective Prompts

✅ **Do:**
- Be specific: "regulation of ready-to-drink alcoholic beverages"
- Include timeframes: "this month", "last 90 days"
- Specify geography: "across different states" or "in the Northeast"
- Mention aspects: "taxation", "licensing", "distribution"

❌ **Don't:**
- Be too broad: "alcohol laws"
- Leave out context: just "beverages"
- Use jargon without explanation

### Organizing Your Research

**Name Your Views Clearly:**
When you create a 360 View, give it a descriptive name so you can find it later.

**Example names:**
- "Ready-to-Drink Alcohol Laws - Q1 2026"
- "Data Center Taxation - January 2026"
- "AI Governance Bills - Winter 2026"

**Build a Knowledge Base:**
- Create monthly 360 Views on your key topics
- Track how legislation evolves over time
- Compare state approaches across quarters

## Understanding the Interface

### Date Range Filter
**Top Right: "Last 90 Days"**
- Control the time window for your analysis
- Options: Last 7 Days, Last 30 Days, Last 90 Days, Custom Range
- Affects all data: bills, hearings, news

### State Filter  
**Top Left: "All"**
- Focus on specific states
- Multi-select available
- Useful for regional analysis

### Activity vs. Sentiment Tabs
**Center Top: "Activity" | "Sentiment"**
- **Activity:** Shows volume (how much is happening)
- **Sentiment:** Shows stance (support vs. opposition)

- Download your 360 View as PDF

## FAQs

**Q: How current is the data in 360 Views?**

A: Data is updated in real-time. When you generate a 360 View, you're getting the latest bills, hearings, and news available in State Affairs.

**Q: Can I edit a 360 View after creating it?**

A: No, but you can create a new 360 View with refined prompts. Previous views are saved for reference.

**Q: How many 360 Views can I create?**

A: Unlimited. All views are automatically saved in your sidebar.

**Q: Can I share 360 Views with my team?**

A: Yes, use the share button to generate a link or download as PDF.

**Q: What states are included?**

A: All 50 states plus Washington D.C. and federal legislation.

**Q: How far back does the data go?**

A: You can analyze data from any time period by adjusting the date range filter.
