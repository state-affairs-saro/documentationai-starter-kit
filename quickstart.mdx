## Overview

This quickstart helps you:

- Sign into State Affairs
- Confirm which states and features you can access
- Take a sensible set of first actions based on your plan

State Affairs supports three main customer types:

- **News Customer**: Non-partisan statehouse news in a single state
- **Platform (Pro + News)**: News plus policy intel such as bills, hearings, schedules, and directories
- **Enterprise**: Platform-like experience across all 50 states, plus AI-enabled tools to find policy signal through noise

If you want more background on the product before you start, see the [Introduction](/introduction).

## Common first steps (all plans)

These steps apply regardless of which plan your organization has.

### Step 1: Sign into State Affairs

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the login page" icon="monitor">

  1. Open your browser.
  2. Go to `https://stateaffairs.com`.
  3. Select the option to log in from the site header.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Enter your credentials" icon="lock">

  1. In the sign-in form, enter your work email associated with State Affairs.
  2. Enter your password.
  3. Select the option to complete sign in.

  If you do not remember your password, use the password reset option on the login screen and follow the prompts.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Confirm successful sign-in" icon="home">

  After a successful login, you will land on your State Affairs experience. Depending on your plan, you may see:

  - A news-focused view for your primary state
  - A policy dashboard with bills, hearings, and schedules
  - A multi-state or national view with additional research tools

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

If you cannot log in or are unsure which email is associated with your account, start with [Accessing State Affairs](/help-center/faq/accessing-state-affairs) or share that article with your internal administrator.

</Callout>

### Step 2: Verify your state access

Your access depends on your plan and how your organization is configured.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Identify your primary state or states" icon="map">

  - If you are a **News Customer**, you should see content for your single covered state.
  - If you are on the **Platform** plan, you should see access to one or more states tied to your subscription.
  - If you are an **Enterprise** customer, you should be able to move between multiple or all 50 states.

  Use the navigation in the product to:

  - Confirm which state or states you can view
  - Switch between states if your plan includes more than one

  </Step>

  <Step title="Check that content is loading as expected" icon="check-circle">

  On your first state view, confirm that you can see:

  - Recent news and updates for that state
  - Any policy or legislative information your plan includes
  - Basic navigation to move between news, policy intel, and any tools available to you

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="info">

If you believe your plan should include additional states or features you do not see, your billing or admin contact can review your subscription in the [Self-Service Portal](/help-center/guides/self-service-portal) and related guides, or contact State Affairs support.

</Callout>

### Step 3: Confirm your role and permissions

Your organization may have multiple users with different roles.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Understand what you should be able to do" icon="users">

  Depending on your responsibilities, you might:

  - Primarily read news and policy updates
  - Monitor specific topics, committees, or offices
  - Manage alerts or tracked items for your team
  - Configure or review billing and seat assignments

  </Step>

  <Step title="Validate access against your responsibilities" icon="shield">

  - If you manage billing or seats, confirm you can reach the Self-Service Portal from your account.
  - If you lead policy monitoring, confirm you can search, filter, and follow the topics or entities you care about.
  - If you are a read-only user, confirm you can access all relevant states and sections you need to monitor.

  </Step>
</Steps>

For help with account-level access and roles, see [Access & Credentials](/help-center/guides/access-credentials).

## News Customer quickstart

This section is for **News Customers**: you pay for non-partisan statehouse news coverage in a single state.

Your first session goal is to:

- Confirm you are seeing the correct statehouse coverage
- Start following the topics, people, or institutions that matter most to you
- Set up a simple monitoring routine

### Step 1: Confirm your statehouse news view

<Steps>
  <Step title="Locate your state news home" icon="home">

  After signing in, navigate to the main news view for your covered state. This view typically emphasizes:

  - Statehouse news and analysis
  - Key political and policy developments
  - Ongoing coverage of the governor, legislature, and major agencies

  </Step>

  <Step title="Scan recent coverage" icon="book-open">

  Spend a few minutes scanning:

  - The most recent headlines and stories
  - Any featured or in-depth pieces
  - Sections that align with your work (for example, specific policy areas, institutions, or offices)

  This gives you a baseline sense of how State Affairs covers your state.

  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2: Focus on your topics and beats

<Steps>
  <Step title="Search for your core topics" icon="search">

  Use the search or filtering options in the news experience to look for:

  - Your organization
  - Key agencies, committees, or offices you track
  - Core policy topics (such as education, transportation, or health)

  Confirm that you can quickly find recent and relevant coverage.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Identify recurring reporters and sections" icon="users">

  As you review search results and sections, note:

  - Which reporters or columns regularly cover your areas of interest
  - Which sections you are likely to check most often

  This helps you build a repeatable reading pattern.

  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 3: Establish a simple monitoring habit

<Steps>
  <Step title="Decide on your check-in cadence" icon="clock">

  Based on your workload and responsibilities, choose how often you will:

  - Open State Affairs to review new stories
  - Scan for mentions of your organization or priorities
  - Share relevant coverage with internal stakeholders

  </Step>

  <Step title="Optionally coordinate with your team" icon="users">

  If multiple people at your organization read State Affairs:

  - Decide who focuses on which topics
  - Agree on how you will share key pieces internally
  - Align on what counts as a "must-read" for leadership

  </Step>
</Steps>

If you later add policy intel or multi-state coverage, you can return to this quickstart and follow the Platform or Enterprise sections.

## Platform quickstart (Pro + News)

This section is for **Platform (Pro + News)** customers: you pay for news plus policy intel such as bills, hearings, schedules, and directories.

Your first session goal is to:

- Confirm access to both news and policy intel for your covered state or states
- Start tracking a small set of high-priority issues
- Make it easier to know what to watch each week

### Step 1: Move between news and policy intel

<Steps>
  <Step title="Verify your news coverage" icon="book-open">

  As in the News Customer quickstart, confirm you can:

  - View your statehouse news home
  - Search for key topics, people, and institutions
  - Identify the sections most relevant to your work

  </Step>

  <Step title="Locate policy intel features" icon="database">

  From your main view, use the product navigation to find areas dedicated to:

  - Bills and legislation
  - Hearings and events
  - Calendars or schedules
  - Directories of offices, legislators, or staff

  You do not need to explore everything immediately. Start by confirming that these sections load and correspond to your state or states.

  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2: Track a small set of issues

<Steps>
  <Step title="Choose 3â€“5 core issues to monitor" icon="target">

  With your team or on your own, decide which issues matter most in the near term. For example:

  - A specific bill or package
  - A committee or agency you watch closely
  - A policy area such as tax, energy, or healthcare

  </Step>

  <Step title="Use search and filters to locate activity" icon="search">

  For each issue, use search and filtering tools in the policy intel sections to:

  - Find related bills and legislative actions
  - Identify relevant hearings or upcoming events
  - See which offices or legislators are most involved

  </Step>

  <Step title="Optional: Mark or follow key items" icon="star">

  If your plan and configuration support it, use any available options to:

  - Mark important bills or entities
  - Save or follow items for quick access later
  - Organize a short list of what you need to watch this week

  Focus on a small number of high-impact items so the signal remains clear.

  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 3: Align news and policy views

<Steps>
  <Step title="Compare policy activity with coverage" icon="zap">

  Take one of your tracked issues and:

  - Look at recent or upcoming legislative activity in the policy intel sections.
  - Search the news view for coverage of the same bills, committees, or themes.

  This helps you see how State Affairs connects inside-the-building activity to public-facing coverage.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide how you will share insights" icon="users">

  Agree on how you will:

  - Flag important developments to internal stakeholders
  - Use both news and policy intel when you brief colleagues or leadership
  - Capture questions that may require deeper research or follow-up

  </Step>
</Steps>

As your team gets comfortable, you can expand to more issues, committees, or states within your subscription.

## Enterprise quickstart

This section is for **Enterprise** customers: you have a Platform-like experience across all 50 states, plus AI-enabled tools designed to find policy signal through noise.

Your first session goal is to:

- Confirm your multi-state access
- Use AI-enabled features to surface signal on a small set of cross-state priorities
- Set up a basic workflow so you are not overwhelmed by volume

### Step 1: Confirm multi-state coverage

<Steps>
  <Step title="Move between multiple states" icon="globe">

  Use the product navigation to:

  - Switch among several states you care about most
  - Confirm you can see both news and policy intel for each state
  - Verify that the content is current and clearly labeled by state

  </Step>

  <Step title="Identify your priority states" icon="map">

  Decide which states are most important for your first week. For example:

  - States where you are active this session
  - States where key policy trends are emerging
  - States where you need to brief leadership soon

  Focus on a small set first so you can build a repeatable pattern.

  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 2: Use AI-enabled tools to find signal

<Steps>
  <Step title="Start from a concrete policy question" icon="help-circle">

  Before using AI features, write down a short, specific question such as:

  - How is a particular policy being handled across several states?
  - Which bills across multiple states touch a certain topic or industry?
  - Where should we focus attention this week on a given issue?

  A focused question makes it easier to evaluate the quality of results.

  </Step>

  <Step title="Apply AI tools to your question" icon="zap">

  Use the AI-enabled capabilities in your Enterprise experience to:

  - Aggregate related news and policy activity across multiple states
  - Highlight which bills, hearings, or actors matter most for your question
  - Reduce repetitive manual searching for similar topics in each jurisdiction

  Pay attention to how the tools explain why particular items are surfaced. This helps you decide how much follow-up review you need.

  </Step>
</Steps>

### Step 3: Build a cross-state monitoring workflow

<Steps>
  <Step title="Define your core watch list" icon="list">

  Based on your first AI-assisted exploration, define:

  - A short list of cross-state issues you will actively monitor
  - Which states you will prioritize for each issue
  - How often you need updates (for example, daily, weekly, or at key calendar moments)

  </Step>

  <Step title="Decide what you will review manually" icon="settings">

  AI tools help reduce noise, but manual review remains important. Decide:

  - Which high-stakes items you will still review directly in the underlying news or policy intel views
  - How you will document or share the key findings with your team
  - When you will revisit your watch list to add or remove topics

  </Step>
</Steps>

<Callout kind="tip">

Enterprise customers often benefit from a short internal guide that lists core questions, priority states, and how your team expects to use AI summaries versus direct source review. Consider drafting and sharing such a guide within your organization.

</Callout>
